Effects of the Typhoon Ondoy

Before we discuss how we can minimize the effects of a typhoon,
let's first discuss its effects on our country.
(Scroll to side to know more)



Loss of homes

During the typhoon Ondoy in Metro Manila, 65 251 buildings were damaged while 44 were completely destroyed.



Distractions of Infrastructures

Two dams discharged water as a result of the severe rain, causing flooding in urban areas. The Magat dam in Ramon, Isabela, and the Angat dam in Norzagaray, Bulacan, are the two dams. It also caused several windows to shatter, causing extensive damage and injuries.



Economic Loss

The damage from typhoon Ondoy is $4.38 dollars. It really caused the economy to go down a significant amount.



Flooding

One of the major effects of typhoon Ondoy is the flooding in Metro Manila and the rest of Luzon. This caused a lot of damage to the infrastructure and agriculture of the Land.



Death and Injuries

This is possibly the most heartbreaking of all. Typhoon Ondoy killed 241 people and injured 394 others. When we are unprepared, a typhoon can be extremely destructive.




Pre-Typhoon Preparation

You just heard on the news that there is a typhoon approaching the PAR, what will you do?
(Scroll to side to know more)



1. Listen to weather forecasts, be attentive


2. Memorize the emergency hotlines


3. Prepare a first aid kit


4. Gather all the essentian documents and papers


5. Check the condition of your house, repair if needed


6. Check all the wiring systems on your house to ensure electrical safety


7. Be prepared for evacuation



Typhoon Preparation/ Actions

Now, you see the rain and hear the strong winds going toward you. What will you do?
(Scroll to side to know more)



1. Calm down, do not Panic


2. Be attentive to news reports/ government announcements


3. Observe your house for any damages


4. Stay in a safe place


5. Secure your important belongings on high-ground


6. Be prepared for evacuation



Post-Typhoon Actions

This time the typhoon is over. You look outside and see trees lying down on the ground with a lot of debris from the distraction of houses. What will you do?
(Scroll to side to know more)



1. Still keep dry and warm


2. Do not approach any wires or outlets


3. Be attentive to the announcements of the government


4. If you are in the evacuation area, stay there and wait for announcements


5. Comfort the people with you


6. Check your houses after the typhoon, if the government allows`
